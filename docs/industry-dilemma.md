### Industry Dilemma: Giant's Technology, Infant's Application

![Web3 Industry Dilemma](/docs/whoBuy.jpg)

#### The "Casino" Swallowed the "Revolution"

We must bluntly point out: **The current crypto industry is deviating from Satoshi Nakamoto's original intention.**

Once upon a time, Web3 promised to reconstruct production relations, grant individuals data sovereignty, and build a trustless, fair world. However, looking at the market today:

- **The Carnival of Vaporware**: Thousands of MEME coins have no practical use, harvesting liquidity solely through emotional incitement and Ponzi-like pumps.
- **PVP Slaughter Games**: Due to the lack of external blood-making capabilities, the industry has fallen into a cruel game of existing funds. So-called "innovations" are mostly designing more complex Ponzi models to see who runs faster.
- **The Closed Loop of Illusion**: DeFi serves DeFi, and DAOs govern thin air. We have built the most complex financial infrastructure in human history, but it seems to be only for trading a few monkey pictures or dog-head tokens.

#### The Wasteland of Trust

Technology is advancing, but the moral water level of the industry is retreating.

- **Hackers and Rug Pulls**: Code vulnerabilities and human greed leave users' assets constantly exposed.
- **The Sword of Damocles of Regulation**: Rampant with money laundering and fraud, Web3 has almost become synonymous with "scam" in the mainstream view. Genuine builders are stigmatized, and real innovations are drowned out by noise.

**We can't help but ask: Is this the Web3 we wanted?**
If blockchain technology cannot solve even one real pain point in the real world and cannot bring positive externalities to society, then it is not only useless but even shameful.

The birth of WikiTruth stems from extreme dissatisfaction with this **"financial idling"**. We refuse to be another on-chain casino; we want to be the scalpel that cuts open the abscess of reality.
